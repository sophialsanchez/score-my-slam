Slam Rules


A slam is a competition between poets, usually played in a series of 3 "bouts." During each bout, poets are scored by judges in the audience on a scale of 0.0 to 10.0. A 0 means that if you could go back in time to any moment in your life to prevent it from happening, it would be to stop yourself from listening to that poem. A 10 means that listening to this poem is the best thing that has ever happened to you in your entire life. Ever.



There aren't many rules surrounding the poet's content. However, poets generally cannot use props or costumes, and the poem performed must be the poet's own work. Lastly, poems should last no more than 3 minutes, with a 10 second grace-period. Poets who go over that time limit will be penalized half a point for each additional 10 seconds he or she uses.

After each round, the highest and lowest scores for each poet are dropped, and an average of the remaining scores is calculated. At the end of the final round, the poet with the highest overall score is declared the winner, with the highest individual score serving as a tie-breaker.

About


Slams are meant to be fun. Though scoring is important to any competition, I made this app to help streamline slams and promote poetry. Love it? Hate it? Got some feedback? Please let me know at sophia@scoremyslam.com

This project was created using Python/Django, HTML/CSS/JQuery, and the Google Maps API.
